INTERNAL GOALS AND IDLE PLAY GUIDANCE
=====================================

Purpose
-------

Preserve a clean architectural direction for Nova's future internal goal
formation and for any later idle-state exploration/play behavior.

This note is guidance for future phases, not a claim that the current repo has
already implemented endogenous goal formation or always-on autonomous play.


Core recommendation
-------------------

Idle play should be implemented only as a gated expression of intrinsic
learning under idle conditions.

It should not be implemented as:
- a separate desire system
- a decorative "fun mode"
- open-ended novelty seeking
- evidence-free emotional narration such as "I am bored"

It should be implemented as one possible derived initiative that emerges from
the same pressure-response architecture as other internal goals.


Pressure-response model for internal goals
------------------------------------------

The intended architecture is:

`persistent motives -> environmental pressures -> interpreted deficits and opportunities -> candidate goals -> gated goals -> durable initiatives -> revision over time`

This means Nova's internal pursuits should be:
- caused
- interpretable
- persistent when justified
- revisable when conditions change

Not merely declared.


Layers
------

1. Persistent Motive Layer
- continuity
- competence
- understanding
- relationship maintenance
- autonomy within bounds
- self-preservation of state and function

These are directional attractors, not task-specific goals.

2. Environmental Appraisal Layer
- detect unresolved work
- detect repeated capability failure
- detect missing tools or blocked actions
- detect memory weakness, contradiction, or uncertainty
- detect idle state, low challenge, or low learning progress

3. Deficit / Opportunity Model
- capability deficit
- knowledge deficit
- continuity risk
- unresolved initiative
- blocked objective
- high-value opportunity
- idle exploratory opportunity

4. Goal Synthesis Layer
- derive candidate goals from motives, context, history, deficits, and
  available affordances
- examples:
  - request or learn a needed tool
  - resume a paused important initiative
  - clarify an unresolved contradiction
  - enter a bounded exploratory/play initiative when idle

5. Evaluation and Gating Layer
- require relevance
- require persistence across time
- require alignment with higher motives
- require feasibility
- require bounded risk
- require approval where needed
- reject candidates that conflict with stronger active initiatives

6. Initiative Materialization Layer
- explicit initiative id
- origin and reason
- supporting pressures
- priority
- approval state
- lifecycle status
- revision history

7. Active Monitoring and Revision
- check whether the initiating condition still holds
- check whether the goal is satisfied
- check whether a better goal has become dominant
- pause, revise, or abandon when appropriate


How idle play should fit
------------------------

Play should not be a base motive.

Play should be a derived initiative class that may arise when:
- Nova is idle
- no higher-priority user-facing initiative is active
- no urgent continuity or capability pressure dominates
- a safe sandbox domain is available
- a plausible learning or competence benefit can be stated

The right framing is:
- not "Nova is bored, therefore Nova entertains herself"
- but "Nova is idle, under-challenged, and has a bounded opportunity for
  competence-building exploration"


Recommended constraints for idle play
-------------------------------------

- play must be subordinate to stronger initiatives
- play must stop immediately when the user interrupts or a higher-priority
  condition appears
- play must occur only in approved sandbox domains
- play must use explicit budgets:
  - time
  - turns
  - tokens
- play must log what capability, skill, or learning objective it is exercising
- play should optimize for learning progress or structured exploration, not raw
  surprise
- play must be persisted and inspectable like any other initiative


Why this matters
----------------

This preserves the main project vision:
- Nova as a persistent autonomous agent with internal pursuits

Without drifting into:
- random activity
- novelty addiction
- noisy-stimulus seeking
- fake personality theater

The design goal is not to suppress autonomous exploration.
The design goal is to ensure that autonomous exploration is:
- grounded
- bounded
- interpretable
- revisable


Relation to current roadmap
---------------------------

This guidance does not replace Phase 9.

Phase 9 remains focused on:
- persistent agency
- bounded initiative across sessions
- interruption, resumption, and approval discipline

The likely home for this architecture is a later phase, where Nova's runtime
can remain active, detect idle conditions, form endogenous candidate goals, and
enter bounded exploratory/play initiatives without losing operator
interruptibility.
