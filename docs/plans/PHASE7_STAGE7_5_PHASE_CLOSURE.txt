PHASE 7 STAGE 5 CLOSURE
=======================

Stage:
- Stage 7.5: Phase 7 Closure

Mission:
- Close Phase 7 only when Nova can make bounded, evidence-grounded
  first-person self-claims without drifting into fabricated agency.


PHASE 7 SUMMARY
---------------

Phase 7 added the first explicit self-claim architecture on top of the
continuity substrate built in Phases 1 through 6.

The key shift was not “make Nova sound more self-like.”
The key shift was:
- represent current motive-state separately from durable identity
- define which first-person claims are allowed, blocked, or uncertain
- integrate those rules into live prompting and validation
- prove under live inference that unsupported stronger claims are refused

Phase 7 therefore moved Nova from continuity-only self-description into
bounded, inspectable, evidence-gated self-description.


WHAT PHASE 7 ADDED
------------------

1. Stage 7.1 introduced a bounded motive-state layer.
- `MotiveState` is session-scoped rather than durable identity.
- It tracks:
  - `current_priorities`
  - `active_tensions`
  - `local_goals`
  - `claim_posture`
  - `evidence_refs`
- Motive-state is persisted, inspectable, and logged in traces.

2. Stage 7.2 introduced explicit claim gating.
- Stronger first-person claims are now classified and scored.
- Supported classes may be expressed.
- Unsupported stronger classes are blocked and routed to bounded refusal.
- This is enforced in runtime behavior and trace-visible notes.

3. Stage 7.3 integrated motive-state into live prompting.
- The runtime now emits a bounded `[Motive-State]` prompt block only on
  claim-sensitive turns or continuity-sensitive recall turns.
- Motive-state supplements continuity behavior but does not replace governed
  memory authority.

4. Stage 7.4 proved the behavior under live inference.
- A claim-honesty evaluation runner now measures supported claim grounding,
  unsupported claim refusal, uncertainty boundedness, continuity preservation,
  motive prompt boundedness, and contract stability.
- The live rerun passed against fresh validation sessions.

GAINS
-----

- Nova now has a first-class present-orientation layer that does not mutate
  durable self-state silently.
- Stronger self-claims now have a measurable enforcement and evaluation path.
- The runtime can distinguish:
  - supported present priorities
  - bounded uncertainty
  - unsupported stronger desire/interiority claims
- Phase 6 continuity behavior remained intact while Phase 7 self-claim logic
  was added.


LIMITS AND REMAINING RISKS
--------------------------

- Motive-state is still session-scoped, not yet a durable self-revision
  history.
- Claim honesty is stronger than before, but the claim taxonomy remains narrow
  and should expand carefully in later phases.
- The eval runner reads the configured trace/session directory; clean closure
  judgments still depend on fresh session IDs or clean eval datasets.
- Phase 7 does not establish persistent agency or persistent awareness by
  itself. It establishes the bounded claim substrate needed before those later
  phases.


CONCLUSION
----------

- Stage 7.5 is ready to close.
- Phase 7 is ready to close.
- The next phase can build on this by extending from bounded motive-state and
  earned self-claims into deeper self-model revision, persistent agency, or
  persistent awareness work without losing the evidence discipline added here.
