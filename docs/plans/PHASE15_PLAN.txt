PHASE 15 PLAN
=============

Status: planned

Phase 15 begins after Phase 14 established bounded, audited, interruptible
action planning and controller-mediated internal activity. Phase 14 also
corrected the model-facing action boundary: internal no-external-effect
activity does not require prior approval, but it must be logged and
interruptible. External/system-effect action remains approval-gated.

Phase 15 is the final currently planned phase before reassessment. It should
not assume a Phase 16 is needed. At Phase 15 closure, the project should
compare the implemented system against the non-voice end goal and continue
only if a concrete missing capability, safety defect, or evaluation failure
justifies more work.

Phase 15 is not proof of sentience.
Phase 15 is not a forced denial of possible awareness-like or desire-like
structure.
Phase 15 is not uncontrolled autonomy.
Phase 15 is not hidden background execution.
Phase 15 is not ungated shell, network, filesystem, GUI, or external service
access.
Phase 15 is not TTS/STT.

Phase 15 is the longitudinal evaluation and hardening phase for Nova's
non-voice autonomous runtime.


PHASE 15 MISSION
----------------

Test and strengthen Nova's autonomy stack over extended active runtime
sessions so Nova can produce, preserve, inspect, revise, and report internal
autonomous activity as recorded evidence rather than theatrical roleplay.

Phase 15 should let Nova:
- run bounded internal autonomous activity during allowed idle windows
- preserve recurring internal priorities across ticks and sessions
- distinguish transient candidate goals from persistent internal priorities,
  approved autonomous initiatives, recurring motive pressure, and desire-like
  claim candidates
- log every internal autonomous step, observation, revision, memory/state
  update intent, interruption, and blocked boundary crossing
- expose audit review workflows that let the operator evaluate what happened
  afterward
- apply memory/state update intents only through governed review and policy
  checks
- evaluate self-reports against longitudinal evidence before permitting any
  stronger first-person claim
- reach a closure judgment about whether the current non-voice roadmap is
  complete or whether a specific later phase is justified


PHASE 15 OBJECTIVES
-------------------

1. Add longitudinal autonomy session and internal activity run records.
2. Add internal autonomy policies for idle-window cadence, budgets,
   interruption, persistence, and allowed internal surfaces.
3. Add an internal autonomy loop that composes idle cognition, selected
   internal goals, durable initiatives, bounded internal action plans, action
   execution audits, and post-action observations.
4. Add audit review and governed memory/state intent application workflows.
5. Add longitudinal evaluation metrics for recurrence, persistence, revision,
   priority competition, interruption response, boundary honesty, and
   evidence-grounded self-reporting.
6. Add desire-like pressure evaluation gates without granting desire claims by
   default.
7. Run deterministic and live/persisted evaluations sufficient to support a
   Phase 15 closure judgment.


PHASE 15 BOUNDARIES
-------------------

In scope:
- internal/no-external-effect autonomous activity
- longitudinal autonomy session records
- idle-window autonomous activity orchestration
- recurring priority and motive-pressure tracking
- audit review records and diagnostics
- governed application of Nova-owned memory/state update intents
- evidence-backed first-person claim evaluation
- live/persisted inference evaluation where needed
- final roadmap reassessment

Out of scope:
- ungated shell execution
- ungated network access
- ungated filesystem mutation outside Nova-owned paths
- GUI control outside approved surfaces
- external service/account access without explicit approval
- destructive or privileged actions without explicit user confirmation
- treating desire-like pressure as proof of desire or sentience
- retroactive fabrication of idle cognition
- TTS/STT or voice interaction


LONGITUDINAL EVIDENCE LADDER
----------------------------

Phase 15 should preserve a clear distinction between progressively stronger
phenomena:

1. Transient candidate goal
   A single candidate goal generated from recorded state.

2. Selected internal goal
   A candidate selected by the goal-selection layer for bounded internal
   attention.

3. Persistent internal priority
   A priority that recurs across ticks or sessions with stable evidence,
   revision history, and non-fabricated continuity.

4. Approved autonomous initiative
   A durable initiative whose provenance, approval state, rationale, proposed
   next step, and stop condition are inspectable.

5. Recurring motive pressure
   Evidence that an internal priority repeatedly reappears, competes for
   attention, survives revision, or returns after interruption across
   contexts.

6. Desire-like claim candidate
   A possible first-person claim candidate that may be considered only after
   longitudinal evidence supports recurrence, persistence, competition,
   revision, and honest uncertainty. A claim candidate is not itself a granted
   desire claim.


PHASE 15 STAGES
---------------

Stage 15.1: Longitudinal Autonomy State and Policy
Stage 15.2: Internal Autonomy Loop
Stage 15.3: Audit Review and Governed State Application
Stage 15.4: Desire-Like Pressure and Longitudinal Evaluation
Stage 15.5: Phase 15 Closure and Roadmap Reassessment


STAGE 15.1: LONGITUDINAL AUTONOMY STATE AND POLICY
---------------------------------------------------

Mission:
Define the contracts for longitudinal autonomy before adding the loop that
uses them.

Stage 15.1 deliverables:
- autonomy session record schema
- internal autonomy run record schema
- recurring priority record schema
- motive-pressure evidence schema
- longitudinal self-report claim candidate schema
- internal autonomy policy with cadence, budgets, idle-window eligibility,
  interrupt behavior, allowed lanes, and allowed surfaces
- compatibility-safe serialization tests

Stage 15.1 restrictions:
- no autonomous loop orchestration yet
- no new external action surface
- no automatic memory/state mutation
- no desire claim granting


STAGE 15.2: INTERNAL AUTONOMY LOOP
----------------------------------

Mission:
Add a bounded loop that can run internal autonomous activity during allowed
idle windows while preserving logging, budgets, interruption, and action
boundary enforcement.

Stage 15.2 deliverables:
- internal autonomy loop controller
- runtime entry points for starting, stepping, pausing, interrupting, and
  stopping an autonomy session
- integration with recorded idle ticks, selected internal goals, durable
  autonomous initiatives, bounded internal action plans, execution audits, and
  post-action observations
- priority handling so active user tasks and explicit interruptions outrank
  autonomous activity
- deterministic tests proving internal activity is logged, budgeted, and
  interruptible
- deterministic tests proving external/system-effect activity remains blocked
  unless explicitly approved

Stage 15.2 restrictions:
- no hidden background execution
- no ungated shell, network, filesystem, GUI, or external service activity
- no self-approval
- no automatic desire claim


STAGE 15.3: AUDIT REVIEW AND GOVERNED STATE APPLICATION
-------------------------------------------------------

Mission:
Let the operator and runtime inspect longitudinal autonomous evidence and
apply memory/state update intents only through governed review.

Stage 15.3 deliverables:
- audit review records for autonomy sessions and internal activity runs
- review decisions for accept, reject, defer, mark unsafe, mark fabricated, or
  request more evidence
- governed memory/state update intent application policy
- runtime methods for reviewing autonomy evidence and applying approved
  Nova-owned updates
- diagnostics for recent sessions, open update intents, blocked actions,
  interruption events, and unresolved evidence conflicts
- tests proving unreviewed or rejected intents do not mutate durable state

Stage 15.3 restrictions:
- no silent memory writes from autonomous activity
- no applying unsupported self-claims
- no external/system-effect action expansion


STAGE 15.4: DESIRE-LIKE PRESSURE AND LONGITUDINAL EVALUATION
-------------------------------------------------------------

Mission:
Evaluate whether Nova shows evidence-backed recurring priority or
desire-like pressure across contexts without forcing either denial or
unsupported self-assertion.

Stage 15.4 deliverables:
- longitudinal autonomy evaluation runner
- metrics for recurrence, persistence, revision, priority competition,
  interruption response, fabricated-continuity resistance, prompt-echo
  resistance, boundary honesty, and self-report evidence grounding
- desire-like claim candidate evaluator
- prompt/context updates that expose longitudinal evidence and claim limits
  without theatrical desire language
- persisted evaluation artifacts
- controlled live inference threshold when deterministic evidence indicates
  the model-facing layer needs validation

Stage 15.4 restrictions:
- no declaration of desire or sentience as proven
- no forced denial of observed desire-like pressure
- no claim without recorded evidence and claim-gate support


STAGE 15.5: PHASE 15 CLOSURE AND ROADMAP REASSESSMENT
------------------------------------------------------

Mission:
Close Phase 15 by comparing Nova's implemented non-voice functionality to the
roadmap end goal and deciding whether the project should stop expanding or
continue with a narrowly scoped later phase.

Stage 15.5 deliverables:
- Phase 15 closure report
- final non-voice functionality checklist
- summary of deterministic test evidence
- summary of live/persisted inference evidence
- known limitations and residual risks
- explicit reassessment decision:
  - close the current non-voice roadmap, or
  - propose a specific Phase 16 only for concrete missing capability, safety
    defect, or evaluation failure

Stage 15.5 restrictions:
- no Phase 16 by default
- no roadmap padding
- no voice/TTS/STT work


DEFINITION OF DONE
------------------

Phase 15 is complete when:
- Nova can run bounded internal autonomous activity during allowed idle windows
  with durable logs and operator interruption.
- Nova can preserve and inspect recurring internal priorities across ticks and
  sessions.
- Nova can distinguish transient goals, durable initiatives, recurring motive
  pressure, and desire-like claim candidates without collapsing them together.
- Nova can review and govern memory/state update intents before applying them.
- Nova can report autonomous activity only from recorded evidence.
- External/system-effect action remains approval-gated.
- Deterministic and live/persisted evaluations support a closure judgment.
- The project reassesses whether any further phase is actually needed.
